# Team Charter

## Goals:
- Goals should focus on desired project outcome (grade/score) and performance objectives of individuals.
    - A (MET) for Grade / High-Quality Outcome/Design
    - Maintain Communication
    - Taking Ownership of Tasks
    - Stand-up meetings live/over Slack at the beginning of the week to delegate tasks and set up goals for the week.

---

## Conflict Management:
- Assume good intentions, keep open communication, maintain professionalism & respectfulness.
    - If any project/assignment-based conflict arises, come to a compromise between members through discussion by clarifying and acknowledging perspectives through active listening and identifying agreements and differences.
    - If no compromise is made by Sundays 5PM, the final call will be made by the Project Manager to continue the progress of the project.
    - Open Communication
    - Come up with a solution, and resolve it
    - Check up after conflict to make sure everybody is still on board

---

## Working Agreement:
- We will hold weekly check-ins/meetings on Saturdays at 10:30am (time flexible when needed).
    - The goal for each individual member is to finish or provide a draft of work based on a task for the corresponding work week by our Saturday meeting time (decided upon in Slack communication).
    - Saturday evenings/Sunday mornings will be an “as-needed” work cushion for any extra work needing to be completed.
    - Any final peer-review comments be shared by Sundays at 12PM. The final submission for each assignment per week will be submitted as a GitHub issue (by Project Manager once approved by Criteria Checker) by Sundays by 8PM.
    - If any prior/life commitments or unexpected matters arise, member(s) will communicate such in Slack and further discussion of assignment/timeline will be made.
- During our live-standup, we should focus on merging code together one-by-one to avoid rebasing and code conflicts.
- Communicate when pushing up code.
